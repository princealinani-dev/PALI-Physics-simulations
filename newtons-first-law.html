📖 Definition & Concept
What is Newton's First Law?

Newton's First Law of Motion, also known as the Law of Inertia, states:

An object at rest will remain at rest, and an object in motion
will remain in motion at constant velocity,
unless acted upon by an external force.

This fundamental principle describes the behavior of objects when no net force acts upon them. It introduces the concept of inertia, which is the resistance of an object to changes in its motion. Objects naturally "want" to stay in their current state—whether moving or stationary.

🔄 Understanding Inertia
What is Inertia?

Inertia is the property of matter that resists changes in its state of motion. The greater the mass of an object, the greater its inertia.

Inertia ∝ Mass
Inertia is directly proportional to an object's mass
Key Concepts About Inertia:
  • Greater mass = Greater inertia (harder to move or stop)
  • Objects naturally resist change in their motion
  • No force needed to maintain constant motion
  • Force is needed to change motion (accelerate, decelerate, or change direction)

🌍 Real-World Examples
Example 1: Seatbelts in Cars

When a car suddenly brakes, your body tends to continue moving forward due to inertia. The seatbelt applies a force to stop your forward motion and keep you safely in your seat.

Example 2: Hockey Puck on Ice

A hockey puck sliding on ice will continue moving in a straight line at constant velocity because the friction force is minimal. Without friction, it would continue indefinitely.

Example 3: Book on a Table

A book resting on a table remains at rest because the gravitational force pulling it down is balanced by the normal force from the table. With no net force, it stays at rest.

Example 4: Spinning Skateboard

If you spin a skateboard and then let it go on a smooth floor, it continues rotating because there's no net torque acting on it—rotational inertia keeps it spinning.

∑ Mathematical Expression
If ΣF = 0, then a = 0
If the sum of all forces (ΣF) equals zero, then acceleration (a) equals zero. The object moves at constant velocity or remains at rest.

What this means:

  • ΣF = sum of all forces acting on the object (N)
  • a = acceleration (m/s²)
  • 0 = no net force and no acceleration
⚙️ Conditions & Applications
When does Newton's First Law Apply?
Conditions:
  • Inertial Reference Frames: The law applies only in non-accelerating reference frames
  • No Net Force: The sum of all forces must equal zero
  • Equilibrium: Object is in equilibrium (static or dynamic)
Applications:
  • 🚗 Vehicle safety systems (airbags, crumple zones)
  • 🛰️ Spacecraft motion in space (no friction, maintains velocity)
  • 🏀 Sports (understanding player and ball motion)
  • ⚙️ Engineering (bridge and building stability)
  • 🎯 Projectile motion analysis
✅ Learning Checklist
🎓 Key Takeaways
Remember These Points:
  • Inertia: Objects resist changes in motion based on their mass
  • At Rest: Objects stay at rest unless a force acts on them
  • In Motion: Objects continue moving at constant velocity unless a force acts on them
  • No Net Force: Means no acceleration (ΣF = 0 → a = 0)
  • Force Required: You need force to change motion, not to maintain it
